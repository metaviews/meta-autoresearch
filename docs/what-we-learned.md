## Purpose

This document synthesizes what the method itself has learned through v1 development, not just what each branch discovered about its domain.

It answers:
1. What epistemic structure types have emerged?
2. What does non-climate portability actually look like?
3. What does the method do well vs. where does it struggle?
4. What artifacts does the method naturally produce?

## Epistemic Structure Types

Three stable structure types have emerged from four research branches. Each structure type names a distinct way that risk or failure becomes visible only through the method's lens.

### 1. Sequence Failure

**Anchor branch:** whiplash (climate volatility)  
**Maturity:** Level 4 (method-shaping)

**Core insight:** Risk becomes visible only when conditions are understood as a **chain** rather than as separate events or seasons.

**How it works:**
- Conventional analysis treats wet seasons and dry seasons as separate problems
- The method reveals that the *transition* between them carries the real risk
- Fast wet-to-dry transitions (or dry-to-wet) create failure modes that neither season alone would predict

**Bounded cases that stabilized the structure:**
- Feather River: wet-to-fire whiplash (flood infrastructure creating fire risk)
- Upper Colorado: recovery-to-shortage whiplash (apparent recovery masking structural shortage)

**What this teaches the method:**
- Sequence structures require temporal framing, not just spatial
- The transition mechanism matters more than the endpoints
- Conventional baselines that treat seasons independently will miss the risk

---

### 2. Correlation/Transmission Failure

**Anchor branch:** breadbasket (climate volatility)  
**Maturity:** Level 4 (method-shaping)

**Core insight:** Risk becomes visible only when distributed nodes are understood as **one coupled system** rather than as separate local problems.

**How it works:**
- Conventional analysis treats regional crop failures as independent events
- The method reveals correlation structures (synchronous failure) and transmission pathways (trade shocks, buffer stress)
- Local problems become systemic when transmission channels amplify rather than absorb

**Bounded cases that stabilized the structure:**
- Russia-Europe wheat trade shock (exporter restriction → importer crisis)
- Russia-China wheat buffer stress (reserve adequacy under correlated stress)
- Egypt vs. Yemen procurement squeeze (differential resilience among importers)

**What this teaches the method:**
- Correlation structures require multi-node analysis
- Transmission pathways can amplify or absorb shocks
- Downstream exposure varies by institutional capacity, not just by import dependence

---

### 3. Design/Rule Conflict Under Volatility

**Anchor branch:** hydrologic (climate volatility)  
**Maturity:** Level 4 (method-shaping)

**Core insight:** Risk becomes visible when inherited operating rules, allocation frameworks, thresholds, or infrastructure purposes **no longer fit** the system dynamics they were built to manage.

**How it works:**
- Conventional analysis assumes stationarity (past baselines predict future conditions)
- The method reveals that infrastructure and rules designed for stationarity fail under non-stationarity
- The conflict is not in the events themselves but in the mismatch between rule architecture and changed conditions

**Bounded cases that stabilized the structure:**
- SEQ water grid and Wivenhoe Dam: rules for drought vs. reality of whiplash flooding
- Upper Colorado Compact: allocation rules for surplus vs. reality of structural shortage

**What this teaches the method:**
- Design/rule conflicts are often invisible to operators inside the system
- The risk is second-order: not the climate event, but the institutional breakdown in response
- This structure type can coexist with sequence failure (hydrologic initially looked like whiplash)

---

## Structure Type Relationships

The three structure types are **not mutually exclusive**. They can coexist in the same domain:

| Relationship | Example |
|--------------|---------|
| **Sequence + Design/Rule** | Hydrologic: whiplash transitions expose infrastructure rules built for stationarity |
| **Correlation + Design/Rule** | Breadbasket: trade shocks expose importer procurement rules built for stable supply |
| **All three** | Potential future branches could surface all structures at different levels |

**Method implication:** Stop forcing branches into single-structure boxes. Hybrid status can be a legitimate intermediate form or even a stable outcome.

## Non-Climate Portability

### The Wealth-Concentration Result

The first non-climate branch (political economy) reached **Level 3 (comparative)** with these characteristics:

- ✅ Multiple grounded variants (US asset-regime, India credit squeeze)
- ✅ Internal comparison structure (evaluation matrix, lead comparison)
- ✅ Explicit curation decisions (keep/revise judgments)
- ✅ Loop-run record with audit trail
- ✅ Discard records with reasoning

**But arrived as a hybrid:** correlation/transmission + design/rule conflict

### Portability Arrives in Stages

| Stage | Description | Wealth-concentration status |
|-------|-------------|----------------------------|
| 1. Thematic similarity | "This also involves risk" | ✅ Passed |
| 2. Structural resonance | "This also has transmission dynamics" | ✅ Passed |
| 3. Hybrid mapping | "This overlaps multiple structures" | ✅ Current state |
| 4. Structure resolution | "This is primarily X, secondarily Y" | ⏳ Not yet |
| 5. Method-shaping | "This changes how we understand structure X" | ⏳ Not yet |

### Key Findings

1. **The method travels beyond climate** — This is no longer an open question. Wealth-concentration demonstrated grounded, curated, auditable work in political economy.

2. **Hybrid status is legitimate** — The method assumed clean structure mapping. Wealth-concentration revealed that non-climate branches may start as hybrids. This is not failure; it's discovery.

3. **Bounded grounding matters more outside climate** — Climate branches could afford broader scenario language early. Non-climate branches need bounded cases earlier to prevent vagueness.

4. **Comparative ≠ Method-shaping** — A branch can demonstrate portability (Level 3) without changing the method itself (Level 4). The distinction matters.

## What the Method Does Well

### Strengths

1. **Resists premature closure**
   - The method consistently generates multiple distinct directions before curating
   - Target fixation is reduced by design (multiple variants, explicit curation)
   - Prediction-centered framing is replaced with scenario-space exploration

2. **Surfaces hidden structures**
   - Sequence failure, correlation/transmission, and design/rule conflict were not pre-existing categories
   - They emerged from grounding in bounded cases
   - The method reveals epistemic structures that conventional analysis misses

3. **Makes curation explicit**
   - Keep/revise/discard decisions are recorded with reasoning
   - Evaluation matrices produce real judgments, not just scores
   - Discard records preserve pruning decisions

4. **Supports cross-domain transfer**
   - Climate volatility proving ground generalized to political economy
   - Structure types are domain-agnostic (sequence, correlation, design/rule)
   - The method can travel without losing rigor

5. **Preserves audit trail**
   - Loop-run records document the full research cycle
   - Branch manifests track state over time
   - Source discipline distinguishes evidence types

## Where the Method Struggles

### Weaknesses and Open Questions

1. **Measurement remains provisional**
   - Unlike ML (validation loss), the method uses qualitative + quantitative scoring
   - Evaluation criteria can drift without explicit discipline
   - Distinguishing "evidence strength" from "scenario value" requires constant vigilance

2. **Scenario proliferation without selection**
   - The method can generate many variants without sufficient pruning pressure
   - Breadbasket reached 5 variants—is there such a thing as too many?
   - Every artifact is kept; soft curation can accumulate overhead

3. **Hybrid resolution is slow**
   - Wealth-concentration remained hybrid through multiple passes
   - The method doesn't yet have a clear "resolve hybrid" pass type
   - Unclear when to accept hybrid vs. push for resolution

4. **Method influence is hard to achieve**
   - Level 4 requires changing the method itself, not just populating it
   - Only 3 of 4 branches have achieved method-shaping status
   - Unclear how many non-climate branches are needed to generalize portability

5. **Token costs limit cycle frequency**
   - ~$0.003 per orchestrated cycle (10-cycle test)
   - Sustainable at current volume, but 10x scale would require optimization
   - Local model support was deferred due to hardware limitations

## What Artifacts the Method Naturally Produces

### Core Artifacts (Every Branch)

| Artifact | Purpose | Maturity Threshold |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Branch manifest** (`meta/branches/<slug>.json`) | Structured state: variants, notes, syntheses, next steps | Required for all branches |
| **Grounding note** (`research/notes/*.md`) | Evidence base for a bounded case | Required for L3+ |
| **Scenario variants** (`research/scenarios/*.md`) | Bounded cases that carry structural signal | 2+ for L3, 3+ for L4 |
| **Comparison synthesis** (`research/syntheses/*.md`) | Distinguishes strongest/weakest variants | Required for L3+ |
| **Loop-run record** (`research/loops/*.md`) | Audit trail of full research cycle | Required for L3+ |
| **Discard record** (`research/discards/*.md`) | Explicit pruning with reasoning | Required for L4 |

### Supporting Artifacts (Mature Branches)

| Artifact | Purpose | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Evaluation matrix** | Scored comparison across 8 dimensions | `2026-03-29-wealth-concentration-evaluation-matrix.md` |
| **Structure mapping** | Tests branch against existing structure types | `2026-03-28-wealth-concentration-structure-mapping.md` |
| **Cross-branch synthesis** | Compares multiple branches to extract method lessons | `2026-03-29-method-lessons-cross-branch-synthesis.md` |
| **Method-level reflection** | What the branch teaches about the method itself | `2026-03-29-non-climate-portability-lessons.md` |

### What Makes Artifacts Useful vs. Ceremonial

**Useful artifacts:**
- Produce real decisions (keep/revise/discard)
- Distinguish strongest from weakest variants
- Change future branch behavior
- Are referenced by later artifacts

**Ceremonial artifacts:**
- Only summarize without sharpening judgment
- Never referenced after creation
- Don't produce curation pressure
- Could be deleted without loss

## What to Stop, Keep, and Start

### Stop Doing

1. **Assuming structure types are mutually exclusive**
   - Hydrologic and wealth-concentration both show overlap is possible
   - Stop forcing branches into single-structure boxes

2. **Treating parent scenarios as method-shaping**
   - The structural signal lives in bounded variants, not parent scenarios
   - Stop evaluating branches by their parent scenario quality

3. **Expecting clean non-climate mapping on first pass**
   - Wealth-concentration shows hybrids are legitimate intermediate forms
   - Stop treating hybrid status as failure

4. **Accumulating without pruning**
   - If every artifact is always kept, curation is too soft
   - Stop generating variants without explicit discard pressure

### Keep Doing

1. **Grounding in named cases**
   - All four branches show this is the path from vague to legible
   - Keep requiring at least 2-3 grounded variants per branch

2. **Explicit curation with keep/revise/discard**
   - The evaluation matrices produce real decisions, not just scores
   - Keep requiring at least one `revise` or `discard` per mature branch

3. **Loop-run records**
   - All four branches have auditable histories
   - Keep requiring loop documentation for L3+

4. **Source discipline**
   - Distinguishing assessed reports, peer-reviewed papers, journalistic reporting
   - Keep preventing eloquent summaries from appearing stronger than evidence base

5. **Human/AI division of labor**
   - AI for generation, remixing, summarization
   - Human for framing, curation, evaluation design
   - Keep judgment explicit and irreplaceable

### Start Doing

1. **Expect and document hybrid structures**
   - Add "hybrid" as a legitimate structure_type value
   - Start requiring structure mapping to test for overlap, not just single-type fit

2. **Cross-branch synthesis every 2-3 branches**
   - This synthesis is the first of its kind
   - Start requiring cross-branch reflection as branches accumulate

3. **Method influence as explicit L4 criterion**
   - Add "influences method documents" to L4 criteria in branch-maturity.md
   - Start tracking which branches change the method vs. populate it

4. **Bounded grounding earlier for non-climate**
   - Non-climate branches need named cases sooner
   - Start requiring grounding notes before variant generation

5. **"What to discard" as explicit pass type**
   - Add `discard` as a first-class run type
   - Start each mature branch with a pruning pass

## Open Questions

### Method Questions

1. **Will wealth-concentration's hybrid status resolve with more grounding?**
   - If yes, hybrid is intermediate
   - If no, hybrid is a stable non-climate form

2. **How many non-climate branches are needed to generalize?**
   - One branch (wealth-concentration) shows portability is possible
   - Two branches would show it's reproducible
   - Three branches would show it's a pattern

3. **What domain should be next for non-climate proving-ground?**
   - Political economy (wealth-concentration) tests one domain
   - A third domain (health? technology? finance?) would test method generality

4. **When does a branch stop expanding and redirect?**
   - Breadbasket has 5 variants—is there such a thing as too many?
   - Should L4 branches have a "maturity ceiling" that triggers redirection?

5. **What would falsify this method?**
   - What evidence would show the method doesn't work?
   - What failure modes should trigger method revision vs. branch pruning?

### Artifact Questions

6. **What is the minimum viable artifact set for a "complete" branch?**
   - Is loop-run + grounding + 2 variants + comparison + discard enough?
   - Or are there additional artifacts required for reusability?

7. **Which artifacts deserve to be published vs. kept internal?**
   - Scenarios? Syntheses? Method lessons?
   - What is the audience for this work?

8. **What tooling would make the method more usable?**
   - Static site for publishing?
   - Structured data model for reusable components?
   - Model orchestration dashboard?
