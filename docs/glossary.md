# Glossary

## Autoresearch

A technical research pattern in which an agent iterates inside a real experimental setup, measures results against a fixed metric, and keeps changes that improve performance.

## Meta-autoresearch

The extension of that epistemological structure beyond machine learning into broader inquiry: stable measurement, open directional exploration, and emergent conclusion.

## Measurement

The criteria used to evaluate whether a scenario, experiment, or synthesis is improving the inquiry. In this project, measurement should be explicit and resistant to motivated drift.

## Search space

The range of plausible directions an inquiry can take. A healthy search space includes possibilities that feel unfamiliar, awkward, or initially unlikely.

## Scenario

A structured representation of a plausible configuration, pathway, or risk pattern. A scenario is not a prediction and should not be read as one.

## Prediction

A narrowed claim about what will happen. In this project, prediction is treated cautiously because premature prediction can compress the search space before the underlying structure is well understood.

## Target fixation

The narrowing of inquiry around a singular expected outcome, forecast, or named objective in a way that suppresses alternative structures, scenarios, or weak signals.

## Curation

The human process of selecting, refining, combining, ranking, or discarding outputs. In this project, curation is part of the method and should be visible.

## Emergence

The appearance of useful structure through iterative contact with evidence, variation, and selection rather than through prior imposition.

## Non-stationarity

The condition in which the underlying distribution generating events is changing, reducing the reliability of simple historical baselines.

## Climate volatility

The shifting and often destabilizing behavior of climate-related conditions, especially where historical norms no longer provide sufficient guidance for preparedness or interpretation.

## Preparedness

A practical orientation toward plausible conditions and risks, especially when prediction is limited and decision-making must proceed under uncertainty.

## Research note

A working document used to capture sources, questions, provisional interpretations, or observations that are not yet mature enough to count as a scenario or synthesis.

## Experiment

A bounded test of method, prompt design, evaluation criteria, curation logic, or scenario format.

## Synthesis

A document that compares, combines, or abstracts across multiple notes, scenarios, or experiments in order to produce a higher-level view.
