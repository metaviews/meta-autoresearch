# Meta-Autoresearch: Foundation

## The problem it addresses

Most inquiry arrives at its destination too early.

We call the process research, but much of the time we are tracing a route toward a conclusion we already hold. We select evidence that confirms familiar patterns, interpret ambiguity in the direction we already prefer, and stop when the resulting picture feels stable enough to live with. This is not usually deception. It is a normal cognitive economy. Pattern recognition is efficient, and in relatively stable systems it often works well enough.

The difficulty is that many of the domains that now matter most are no longer stable in that way. The system has moved. Our inquiry often has not.

## From autoresearch to meta-autoresearch

In its original technical form, autoresearch refers to a setup in which an AI agent is given a real experimental environment and allowed to iterate inside it autonomously. It runs experiments, measures outcomes against a fixed metric, keeps what improves the result, discards what does not, and repeats. The process does not begin by defending a preferred answer. It follows measured improvement.

Three design principles matter:

- a fixed measurement that does not move
- no predetermined direction inside the search space
- accumulated emergence, where useful structure is discovered through iteration rather than imposed from the start

Meta-autoresearch takes that epistemological structure and applies it more generally. The scaling move is not mainly about larger compute or broader automation. It is about recognizing that many consequential forms of inquiry fail for the same reason: they resolve too soon.

## The posture

The method asks the same three questions in every domain:

1. What is the most rigorous measurement available, and how do we keep it from drifting?
2. What is the real space of possible directions, including those that are uncomfortable or counterintuitive?
3. What process allows structure to emerge instead of forcing a conclusion in advance?

The posture is demanding because it requires openness without laxity. Not every angle is equally persuasive. But in the early phases of inquiry, many more angles should be taken seriously than most institutions are willing to entertain. The point is not relativism. The point is to avoid cutting off signal before it has had a chance to appear.

## Emergence, humility, and disciplined openness

This project treats emergence as a practical research stance. In complex systems, especially those with unstable baselines and interacting feedback loops, the answer cannot always be read directly from prior models or familiar narratives. It has to be discovered through a process that is strict about evidence and honest about surprise.

That requires humility, but not passivity. The discipline lies in constructing a process that can report what it actually finds, even when the findings do not flatter our preferences or fit our existing frames.

## Why climate volatility comes first

Climate volatility is the first proving ground because it concentrates the exact failures this method is designed to address.

Public understanding of climate risk still leans heavily on historical continuity: seasonal memory, thirty-year normals, inherited assumptions about planning, and institutions built around stationary baselines. Those assumptions are under growing strain. The issue is not simply that the future is uncertain. It is that the underlying distribution appears to be moving.

That makes a narrow predictive posture insufficient. What is needed first is disciplined scenario work: the construction of plausible configurations of what could happen, without forcing the system into a single story too early.

Meta-autoresearch is meant to support that work. AI can help generate, vary, and recombine scenarios. Human judgment remains necessary for curation, pattern recognition, and deciding which threads deserve deeper development.

## What this repository is for

This repository is a visible working space for developing the method before making larger claims for it.

That means:

- clarifying the conceptual foundations
- turning the posture into an operational workflow
- stress-testing the method against climate volatility research questions
- documenting where the method succeeds, fails, or remains underspecified

The aim is not to present a finished doctrine. It is to build an honest research practice around a promising stance.

## Tone and disposition

The project takes rigor seriously without collapsing into false solemnity. Complex systems are often alarming, but they are also strange, nonlinear, and occasionally absurd in ways that simplistic seriousness cannot hold well. A useful research posture can remain alert, exacting, and open to surprise without becoming either detached or melodramatic.

That tone matters because playfulness, used carefully, can be part of rigor. It keeps inquiry open long enough for unexpected structure to appear.
