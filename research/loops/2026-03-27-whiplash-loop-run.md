## Purpose of this run

Document the first full whiplash cycle from broad scenario generation through revision, regional grounding, variant comparison, evaluation, and cross-branch method lessons.

## Stage 1: Frame the inquiry

- research question:
  - How might climate volatility produce preparedness failure not mainly through one catastrophic event, but through a sequence in which apparent relief or one hazard mode is followed by a different stress mode that institutions fail to interpret as part of the same chain?
- boundaries:
  - focus on sequence failure and institutional misreading rather than prediction
  - keep the branch in climate volatility and preparedness, not total social collapse
  - avoid treating every multi-hazard story as whiplash unless the transition itself is the main failure mechanism
- why this inquiry now:
  - the initial climate pass surfaced sequence blindness as one of the strongest early mechanisms
  - this branch became the main test of whether the method could discover temporal failure structures rather than only topical clusters
- anti-target-fixation check:
  - the branch was framed around plausible transition patterns, not around predicting one canonical future event
- initial unit of work:
  - one broad scenario family later revised into named regional variants

- robust steps in this run:
  - family revision under evaluation pressure
  - named-case grounding
  - comparison of distinct temporal mechanisms
  - cross-branch structural interpretation
- brittle steps in this run:
  - direct evidence of institutional interpretation failure is still somewhat weaker than the structural pattern itself
  - no explicit discard log yet
- where stronger models were genuinely needed:
  - early family reframing and branch comparison synthesis
  - higher-order structural interpretation of the branch as sequence failure

## What failed or was pruned

- discarded directions:
  - broad hazard-cluster framings that did not make the temporal transition itself the main object of analysis
- merged directions:
  - some general wet-to-hot-dry language now functions as the parent family rather than as a stand-alone final artifact
- places where the loop produced noise, drift, or overclaiming:
  - early tendency to bundle too many hazards together under one narrative
  - risk of implying stronger local causal chains than the evidence currently supports

## Method lessons from this run

- what worked:
  - evaluation pressure improved the parent family materially
  - named regional variants made the branch far more legible
  - comparison clarified that whiplash is really about temporal epistemic failure
- what changed in the method:
  - the branch helped reveal `sequence failure` as a reusable structure type
  - the method now better distinguishes family-level revision from variant-level keep decisions
- what should be done differently next time:
  - document rejected sequence framings more explicitly
  - gather operator-facing and chronology-level evidence earlier in the cycle

## Next move

- immediate next step:
  - use the branch to judge whether future candidate cases genuinely instantiate sequence failure rather than only generic compounding
- longer follow-up:
  - return to the hydrologic branch and test whether it becomes a sibling or subtype relative to whiplash
- scaling relevance:
  - this run is partially repeatable, but would benefit from stronger evidence-ingestion support before large-scale repetition
