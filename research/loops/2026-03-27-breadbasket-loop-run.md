## Purpose of this run

Document the first full breadbasket cycle from broad scenario family through grounding, variant creation, comparison, evaluation, and method lessons.

## Stage 1: Frame the inquiry

- research question:
  - How might climate volatility create preparedness failure through correlated stress across major food-producing regions rather than through a single local crop disaster?
- boundaries:
  - focus on scenario exploration rather than prediction
  - treat climate as the initiating stressor, but keep trade, policy, and importer exposure inside scope
  - avoid claims of total global crop collapse
- why this inquiry now:
  - the branch emerged early as one of the strongest alternatives to purely local or sequential climate framing
  - food-system fragility offered a strong test of correlation and transmission logic
- anti-target-fixation check:
  - the branch was framed around a family of possible system failures, not around a single forecasted food crisis
- initial unit of work:
  - scenario family, later narrowed into crop- and node-specific variants

## What failed or was pruned

- discarded directions:
  - broad food-crisis language that did not specify correlation, transmission, or preparedness mechanisms
- merged directions:
  - generic northern wheat stress now functions as a parent branch rather than as an isolated final scenario
- places where the loop produced noise, drift, or overclaiming:
  - early tendency to imply global scarcity rather than unequal access
  - tendency to blur physical stress with policy amplification before the branch was explicitly split

## Method lessons from this run

- what worked:
  - focused branch deepening produced clearer structures than adding many new families
  - comparison sharpened curation more than polishing did
  - source-backed notes materially improved the branch
- what changed in the method:
  - branch-level comparison is now a real method component, not an optional extra
  - evaluation and synthesis can identify different node roles inside the same family
- what should be done differently next time:
  - document discard decisions more explicitly
  - add downstream preparedness layers earlier rather than treating them as a final add-on

## Next move

- immediate next step:
  - compare how the Russia-Europe and Russia-China variants differ at the importer, reserve, and procurement level
- longer follow-up:
  - test whether wheat remains the best lead crop or whether another crop branch becomes more decision-useful
- scaling relevance:
  - this run is a good candidate for future partial repetition because its stages are now visible and many sub-tasks are bounded enough for later delegation
