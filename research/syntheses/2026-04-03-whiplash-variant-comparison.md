## Purpose

This synthesis compares all whiplash variants across the agentic/non-agentic dimension to test whether the sequence failure structure holds across climate sequences, policy sequences, and hybrid agentic/non-agentic sequences.

**Questions this answers:**
1. Does sequence failure hold across non-agentic (climate) and agentic (policy) sequences?
2. Is hybrid sequence (climate policy) more dangerous than pure sequences or just different?
3. What subtypes emerge from this comparison?

## Agentic/Non-Agentic Comparison

### Non-Agentic Sequences (Climate)

**Variants:** Feather River wet-to-fire, Upper Colorado recovery-to-shortage

**Shared mechanisms:**
1. Transition is the risk — Not the endpoints but the shift between conditions
2. Optimization for one regime — Actors optimize for current climate, creating vulnerability
3. Separate planning categories — Wet/fire or recovery/shortage planned as independent "seasons"
4. Adaptation lag — Infrastructure rules optimized for one regime malfunction under the other
5. No beneficiaries — No documented actors benefit from climate sequence failure

**Key difference between the two:**
- Feather River: Fire risk amplification (wet creates fuel, dry creates ignition)
- Upper Colorado: Recovery deception (apparent recovery masks structural shortage)

**Judgment:** ✅ **Both are clear sequence failure cases.** Both show transition-as-risk, separate planning, adaptation lag.

---

### Agentic Sequence (Policy)

**Variant:** Policy regime whiplash

**Mechanisms:**
1. Transition is the risk — Not expansion or contraction but the shift between regimes
2. Optimization for one regime — Actors optimize for current policy, creating vulnerability
3. Separate planning categories — Expansion/contraction or deregulation/reregulation planned independently
4. Adaptation lag — Capital/compliance optimized for one regime malfunction under the other
5. **Beneficiaries exist** — Some actors gain from whiplash (arbitrage, political advantage, competitive displacement)

**Key difference from non-agentic:**
- Policy can reverse quickly (election, executive order) while infrastructure cannot
- Beneficiaries exist who actively shape transitions
- Partial predictability (elections are scheduled; crises are not)

**Judgment:** ✅ **Sequence failure structure holds** for agentic sequences, but with modifications: faster reversibility, beneficiaries, partial predictability.

---

### Hybrid Sequence (Climate Policy)

**Variant:** Climate policy whiplash

**Mechanisms:**
1. **Dual transition risk** — Both climate transition (wet-to-dry) AND policy transition (mitigation-to-adaptation) create failure
2. **Asymmetric reversibility** — Policy reverses quickly; infrastructure cannot — creating compounding stress
3. **Dual unpredictability** — Climate becoming less predictable AND political transitions uncertain
4. **Compounding adaptation** — Must adapt infrastructure AND policy compliance simultaneously
5. **Multiple beneficiary types** — Both climate arbitrage and political advantage beneficiaries

**Key difference from pure sequences:**
- More dangerous than either pure sequence alone (compounding, not additive)
- Asymmetric reversibility creates unique failure mode (policy shifts but infrastructure remains misaligned)
- Dual unpredictability multiplies risk, not adds

**Judgment:** ✅ **Hybrid sequence is more dangerous than pure sequences** — not just different, but compounding risk through asymmetric reversibility and dual unpredictability.

## Method Lesson

**What this teaches the method:**

1. **Sequence failure generalizes across agentic/non-agentic drivers** — The core mechanism (transition-as-risk, separate planning, adaptation lag) holds for both climate and policy sequences.

2. **Hybrid sequences are more dangerous than pure sequences** — Asymmetric reversibility and dual unpredictability create compounding risk that neither pure sequence produces alone.

3. **Subtype parameters can be quantified** — Intervention point density, evolutionary feedback, compounding factor, and danger level can distinguish sequence subtypes.

4. **Beneficiary analysis matters for agentic sequences** — Unlike climate sequences (no beneficiaries), policy sequences create winners who actively shape transitions. The method should track beneficiary dynamics for agentic sequences.
