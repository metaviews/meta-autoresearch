## Purpose

Compare the importer and reserve implications of the two leading wheat variants in the breadbasket branch:

- `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-russia-europe-wheat-trade-shock.md`
- `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-russia-china-wheat-buffer-stress.md`

The aim is to make the downstream preparedness layer more operational by asking not only which upstream mechanism is stronger, but what each one means for different importer types.

## Main distinction

### Russia-Europe variant

This is the cleaner `trade-buffer failure` case.

- one major export node is stressed
- one major substitute export buffer is also weakened
- procurement conditions tighten quickly
- importers feel the shock through price formation, tender competition, and reduced fallback supply

### Russia-China variant

This is the cleaner `production-buffer failure` case.

- one major export node is stressed
- one major domestic production-and-stock system becomes less reliable as a hidden buffer
- the world market tightens more indirectly through stock retention, domestic balancing, or added import demand
- importers feel the shock less through immediate substitution failure and more through thinner background slack

## Importer-side implications

### Egypt

Best fit: `Russia-Europe`

Why:

- Egypt is a scale procurement case
- imports cover more than half of annual consumption
- tendering, procurement prices, and subsidy burden matter immediately when tradable wheat supply tightens

The Russia-Europe variant is more operational for Egypt because it directly stresses the tradable export corridor and substitute buffer logic that procurement systems depend on.

### Jordan

Best fit: mixed, but short-run less acute in both variants

Why:

- Jordan is highly import dependent
- but strategic stocks equivalent to about ten months of consumption materially change short-run exposure

Jordan is useful as a resilience test case. It shows that import dependence alone does not determine immediate vulnerability. Reserve depth matters.

### Lebanon

Best fit: `Russia-Europe`, with strong vulnerability overlay

Why:

- Lebanon's wheat-import needs remain significant
- household and system fragility amplify procurement stress quickly

For Lebanon, the cleaner and faster trade-disruption logic of Russia-Europe is more immediately relevant than the slower and more inferential Russia-China buffer-loss story.

### Yemen

Best fit: both, but via different pathways

Why:

- Yemen is the strongest procurement-sensitivity case in the current note set
- FX access, importer capacity, and acute insecurity make it highly exposed even before absolute scarcity is reached

In the `Russia-Europe` variant, Yemen is exposed to immediate procurement tightening.

In the `Russia-China` variant, Yemen is exposed to a thinner and more expensive market background, especially if large-system buffering weakens and import competition rises.

### Tunisia

Best fit: `Russia-Europe`, but as a middle case

Why:

- Tunisia is neither as buffered as Jordan nor as acutely fragile as Yemen
- it is a useful case for seeing how tradable supply tightening affects a partially import-dependent state without collapsing into a crisis-only frame

## Reserve logic comparison

### Russia-Europe

Reserve relevance is immediate.

If substitute export capacity narrows quickly, countries with thin reserves or weak procurement flexibility are hit first. This makes reserve duration and tender agility central variables.

### Russia-China

Reserve relevance is more indirect but still important.

If a large domestic buffer becomes less reliable, the global market loses hidden slack. That makes reserve assumptions more important over a slightly longer horizon, especially for countries that cannot compete well when background tightness rises.

## Working judgment

### Best immediate preparedness variant

`research/scenarios/2026-03-27-russia-europe-wheat-trade-shock.md`

Why:

- it maps more directly onto procurement stress
- it is easier to connect to importer and reserve decisions in the current evidence base
- it offers a clearer first branch for importer-facing preparedness work

### Best secondary or systemic background variant

`research/scenarios/2026-03-27-russia-china-wheat-buffer-stress.md`

Why:

- it remains valuable for showing how hidden global slack can weaken
- it is especially useful for understanding medium-horizon vulnerability and reserve assumptions
- it is currently less direct but more structurally interesting in system terms

## Importer archetypes that emerge

The current note set suggests at least four downstream archetypes:

- `scale procurement state` - Egypt
- `import-dependent but reserve-buffered state` - Jordan
- `fragile importer with high household vulnerability` - Lebanon
- `procurement-constrained acute-risk importer` - Yemen

Tunisia functions as a useful middle case rather than a separate archetype.

## What this changes in the branch

- the breadbasket branch is now more clearly usable for importer-facing preparedness work
- Russia-Europe should remain the lead operational variant for next-pass downstream work
- Russia-China should remain active as the branch's broader system-buffer comparison rather than replacing the lead variant
- future branch deepening should likely focus on importer archetypes rather than adding more upstream node variants immediately
