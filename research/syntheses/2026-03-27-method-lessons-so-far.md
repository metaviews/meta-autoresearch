## Purpose

Capture what the first research cycles have actually taught the project about the method itself: where the loop is working, where it drifts, and what should be preserved before any attempt to scale it.

## Main judgment

The method is working best when it moves through a visible sequence:

1. broad scenario generation
2. evidence grounding
3. branch formation
4. internal comparison
5. explicit evaluation
6. synthesis at a higher structural level

When any of those stages are skipped, the work becomes noticeably weaker.

## Lesson 1: Single-pass generation is useful, but not trustworthy by itself

The first scenario pass was valuable because it produced a concrete artifact quickly. But it also showed a recurring risk: the first coherent narrative can sound better than it deserves.

What seems true now:

- first-pass generation is good for surfacing mechanisms
- it is weak as a stopping point
- it needs comparison quickly, otherwise eloquence outruns method

## Lesson 2: Comparison improves the method more than polishing does

The early shift from one scenario to multiple scenario families was one of the most important improvements in the repo.

Why:

- comparison forces real curation
- comparison reveals whether two artifacts are actually distinct or just rhetorically different
- comparison shows which branches deserve more evidence and which should stay provisional

This is now visible in both the `whiplash` and `breadbasket` branches.

## Lesson 3: Evidence notes are the bridge between philosophy and useful scenarios

The work improved substantially once source-backed notes entered the loop.

Without notes:

- scenarios tend to become too smooth
- causal claims become too easy to imply
- uncertainty remains underdescribed

With notes:

- grounding becomes explicit
- scenario claims can be bounded more honestly
- syntheses can compare evidence-rich and evidence-thin directions more clearly

## Lesson 4: Branches are more useful than isolated scenarios

The repo is no longer best understood as a set of individual scenarios. It is becoming a set of branches with internal logic.

That matters because branches support:

- regional variants
- mechanism comparison
- cumulative grounding
- clearer pruning decisions

This is one of the strongest signs that the method is becoming reusable.

## Lesson 5: Evaluation changes behavior

Adding the evaluation framework and matrix was not just a documentation step. It changed the work.

Once `keep`, `revise`, `merge`, and `discard` became explicit:

- weaker branches could be kept provisionally without being treated as equal
- stronger branches could justify more focused deepening
- evidence strength and scenario value could be distinguished instead of blurred

The method got more honest as soon as evaluation became public inside the repo.

## Lesson 6: Status-quo challenge and imaginative power are real methodological criteria

Adding those dimensions clarified something the project already implicitly cared about.

The method is not only trying to generate plausible outputs. It is trying to generate disciplined alternatives to institutional narrowing.

That means good scenarios often do two things at once:

- they remain bounded enough to be criticizable
- they widen what can be seriously considered

This appears to be central, not optional.

## Lesson 7: Different branches reveal different epistemic structures

The work now suggests that the method is detecting more than topic clusters.

So far, at least two structures have emerged:

- `sequence failure`
- `correlation/transmission failure`

This may be one of the most important developments so far. If it holds, the method becomes more than a scenario workflow. It becomes a way of identifying recurring forms of hidden systemic legibility.

## Lesson 8: Scale is not the next step; cycle quality is

The repository is making progress, but the strongest signal is not "we are ready to automate more." The strongest signal is "we are beginning to understand which parts of the loop deserve to be repeated."

What seems necessary before scale:

- stronger case chronology in weaker branches
- clearer importer and reserve comparisons in stronger branches
- more explicit records of what the method should stop doing
- at least one more round of cross-branch comparison

## What the method currently does well

- turns abstract framing into structured research artifacts
- supports branch formation rather than only one-off outputs
- makes curation and evaluation more visible than typical exploratory work
- encourages surprise without surrendering discipline

## What still looks weak

- some branches still rely on broad plausibility more than case-level proof
- the repo has more `keep` and `revise` decisions than true `discard` decisions
- the method has not yet documented enough failed directions to prove its pruning strength
- the reusable loop template now exists, but it is still young and not yet tested across enough domains or branch types

## Current conclusion

The project has crossed an important threshold.

It is no longer mainly testing whether the concept can produce interesting material. It is now testing whether the concept can produce a disciplined, repeatable inquiry pattern that improves what gets noticed, compared, and kept.

That is real progress.

## Next move

Use the current state of the repo to keep refining the working loop more explicitly, including:

1. branch creation
2. branch grounding
3. branch comparison
4. branch evaluation
5. cross-branch synthesis

If that loop can be pressure-tested cleanly across more branch types, the project will be much closer to a cycle that can later be repeated with lower-cost models.
