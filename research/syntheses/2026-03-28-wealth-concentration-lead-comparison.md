## Purpose

Directly compare the two strongest current leads inside the wealth concentration branch:

- `US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In`
- `India Last-Mile Credit Squeeze`

The aim is to test whether the branch is beginning to resolve toward one dominant structure or whether the hybrid remains the more honest description even after a stronger downstream transmission case has been added.

## The two lead candidates

### 1. US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In

Primary structural read:

- `design/rule conflict under volatility`

Core logic:

- tax and transfer rules fit housing-heavy middle wealth better than the financial and business asset forms through which concentrated wealth compounds

### 2. India Last-Mile Credit Squeeze

Primary structural read:

- `correlation/transmission failure`

Core logic:

- concentrated funding and non-bank intermediation shape where credit withdrawal lands, with smaller NBFCs/MFIs and downstream borrowers squeezed first when confidence tightens

## Main comparison

### Which is more bounded and legible now?

`US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In`

Why:

- the institutional mechanism is clearer
- the rule mismatch is more directly documented
- it is easier to identify what exactly no longer fits the asset structure of concentrated wealth

### Which is more downstream and system-propagation oriented?

`India Last-Mile Credit Squeeze`

Why:

- it follows fragility farther down the chain to smaller lenders, micro borrowers, and MSME credit access
- it gives the branch a more concrete transmission pathway than the earlier private-credit case alone

### Which is the stronger current proving-ground lead?

Still `US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In`.

Why:

- it remains the most bounded and policy-legible case
- it offers the clearest portability test of `design/rule conflict under volatility`
- it still carries less ambiguity about what mechanism is central

### Which now competes more seriously than before?

`India Last-Mile Credit Squeeze`.

Why:

- it is much less abstract than the earlier broad transmission framing
- it shows how concentrated funding structures can matter for downstream resilience in a visible, non-elite setting
- it makes the hybrid interpretation harder to dismiss as indecision

## Working judgment

The branch still remains best described as `hybrid`.

But the balance has changed:

- `design/rule conflict` remains the stronger bounded lead
- `correlation/transmission failure` is now a substantially stronger competitor than it was before

That is important. The branch is not simply failing to choose. It is showing that two different structural grammars genuinely illuminate different parts of the domain.

## Method implication

This is a stronger portability result than the earlier comparison.

Why:

- the wealth-concentration branch now contains a serious rule-centered case and a serious downstream transmission case
- both are legible enough to support real structural comparison
- the method can now say, with more confidence, that new domains may remain hybrid not because they are vague, but because multiple failure structures are genuinely operative

## Next move

Do not force resolution yet.

Instead:

1. keep the branch explicitly hybrid for now
2. use one more pass to decide whether the next priority is:
   - another rule-centered case,
   - another transmission case, or
   - a loop/maturity judgment for the non-climate branch as a whole

My recommendation is the third: move to a maturity pass next rather than adding another immediate case.
