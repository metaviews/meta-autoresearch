## Purpose

Compare the two current framings inside the wealth concentration proving-ground branch:

- `research/scenarios/2026-03-28-wealth-concentration-structural-stress.md`
- `research/scenarios/2026-03-28-us-asset-regime-and-wealth-lock-in.md`

The aim is to determine whether the branch is better led by a broad balance-sheet/transmission framing or by a more bounded asset-regime and tax-design mismatch framing.

## The two current framings

### 1. Wealth Concentration Structural Stress

Core mechanism:

- concentration compounds because top wealth holds scalable assets, earns higher returns, and shapes the channels through which fragility moves

Best current interpretation:

- broad `balance-sheet / transmission` framing

### 2. US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In

Core mechanism:

- rule architecture and asset architecture no longer fit cleanly; taxation and transfer rules map more directly to housing-heavy middle wealth than to financial and business asset structures through which concentrated wealth compounds

Best current interpretation:

- bounded `design/rule conflict` framing

## Main comparison

### Breadth vs boundedness

- `Wealth Concentration Structural Stress` is more generative because it opens multiple channels at once: returns, portfolio composition, privatization, deregulation, and hidden intermediation
- `US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In` is more bounded because it ties the branch to a concrete institutional mismatch with publicly legible rule structures

The trade-off is now clear: the first framing is wider and more structurally suggestive, while the second is narrower and more directly testable.

### Stronger current evidence fit

The stronger current fit is `US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In`.

Why:

- the note and scenario are grounded in a clearer institutional mechanism
- the asset-regime mismatch can be described with more specific evidence than the broader transmission framing currently can
- it gives the branch a better immediate proving-ground case for non-climate portability

### More generative long-run framing

The more generative framing remains `Wealth Concentration Structural Stress`.

Why:

- it keeps open the possibility that hidden transmission and balance-sheet intermediation may ultimately become the deeper dominant mechanism
- it avoids narrowing the branch too early around tax design alone

## Working judgment

The best current read is:

- the branch remains `hybrid`
- but its best grounded leading edge is now `design/rule conflict under volatility`

That is not a contradiction.

It means:

- the branch's broad explanatory horizon is still hybrid
- its most bounded and currently defensible sub-branch is the asset-regime mismatch case

## Method implication

This is a valuable portability result.

It shows that in a new domain:

- the first framing may remain broader and more generative
- but progress may still come by grounding the branch first in the mechanism that yields the clearest case

In other words, portability does not require immediate purity. It requires disciplined narrowing without losing the larger structural horizon.

## Next move

The next pass should not widen the branch again immediately.

Instead:

1. keep `US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In` as the current strongest bounded case
2. identify one contrasting transmission-focused case inside wealth concentration
3. then compare those two sub-branches directly

That will tell us whether wealth concentration resolves toward one dominant structure or remains a meaningful hybrid.
