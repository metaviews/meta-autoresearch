# Non-Climate Portability Lessons

## Metadata

- title: Non-Climate Portability Lessons
- date: 2026-03-29
- author: OpenCode
- status: draft
- type: method synthesis

## Purpose

Capture what the `wealth-concentration` proving-ground branch teaches the method about cross-domain portability.

The key question is no longer whether the method can leave climate at all. It can. The stronger question is what kind of evidence, branch development, and structural clarity are required before non-climate portability should be treated as method-shaping rather than merely promising.

## Main judgment

The first non-climate result is real but not final.

`wealth-concentration` is now strong enough to demonstrate `comparative portability`, but not yet strong enough to demonstrate `method-shaping portability`.

That distinction matters.

## What has now been demonstrated

The method has shown that outside climate it can still:

- resist obvious surface narratives
- identify hidden structure
- ground a parent branch in multiple bounded cases
- compare competing structural interpretations
- make explicit `keep`, `revise`, and `discard` pressure
- preserve the work through loop runs, manifests, and syntheses

That is enough to say the method travels.

## What did not happen

The branch did not map neatly to a single existing structure type on the first or even second pass.

Instead it behaved like this:

1. broad structural fit
2. bounded rule-conflict case
3. bounded transmission case
4. internal comparison
5. explicit branch-level judgment that the branch remains hybrid

This is not a failure of transfer. It is the first serious evidence of how transfer may actually work.

## Portability lesson 1: Hybrid status can be a real stage, not just indecision

The climate branches matured into clearer leading structures relatively quickly.

The first non-climate branch did something different. It remained a meaningful hybrid between:

- `correlation/transmission failure`
- `design/rule conflict under volatility`

That suggests non-climate transfer may often begin in overlap rather than in clean structural separation.

## Portability lesson 2: Bounded cases matter more outside the original proving ground

In climate, the method could afford some broader scenario language early because the proving ground itself was already highly coherent.

Outside climate, broad framing becomes weaker much faster.

The wealth branch only became credible once it produced bounded cases like:

- `US Asset-Regime and Wealth Lock-In`
- `India Last-Mile Credit Squeeze`

This suggests non-climate branches need bounded grounding earlier than climate branches may have.

## Portability lesson 3: Comparative portability is a real threshold

The branch is now `Level 3: comparative proving-ground run`.

That is not trivial.

It means a domain outside climate can already support:

- multiple grounded variants
- internal comparison
- pruning pressure
- loop auditing
- structural interpretation

This is enough to justify continued non-climate work, even if it is not yet enough to reshape the method itself.

## Portability lesson 4: Method-shaping portability demands more than successful transfer

What the climate Level 4 branches did was not only travel well. They changed the method.

The wealth branch has not yet done that.

It has strengthened confidence that the method travels, but it has not yet forced a revision of:

- the structure-type vocabulary
- the loop
- the maturity model
- the evaluation framework

That is why it remains Level 3.

## Portability lesson 5: Cross-domain validation should look for new demands, not just familiar wins

The wealth branch has already placed different demands on the method than climate did.

It required:

- faster bounded grounding
- more tolerance for durable hybrid status
- earlier branch-specific evaluation
- more explicit separation between a broad structural umbrella and the strongest bounded lead

Those are not bugs. They are signals about what portability really requires.

## What now appears required for non-climate Level 4

For a non-climate branch to become method-shaping, it likely needs to do at least one of these:

- force a revision of an existing structure type
- stabilize a new structure type or sub-type strongly enough to change the method documents
- reveal a new loop requirement that climate branches did not expose
- reshape the maturity or evaluation model in a durable way

The current wealth branch has not yet done that.

## Working conclusion

The method now has a meaningful cross-domain threshold:

- `Level 3 non-climate portability` means the method can travel, ground itself, compare internally, and remain epistemically honest outside climate
- `Level 4 non-climate portability` would mean the new domain changes the method, not just validates it

We are not there yet.

But we are no longer guessing about portability either.

## Next implication

The project should not rush to many new non-climate domains yet.

The better move is to:

1. understand what made wealth concentration portable but not yet method-shaping
2. decide whether one more pass might change that
3. only then choose whether to deepen wealth further or test a second non-climate proving ground
