## Purpose

Compare the first three climate-volatility scenario families in the repo, identify what each one contributes, and decide which family is most worth deepening next.

## The three families so far

### 1. Sequencing failure

Primary artifact: `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-compound-seasonal-whiplash.md`

Core mechanism: the main risk comes from rapid transitions between conditions that institutions still treat as separate seasonal problems.

Best use: testing preparedness failure caused by lagging categories, messaging, and operational sequencing.

### 2. Correlated breadbasket stress

Primary artifact: `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-synchronous-breadbasket-stress.md`

Core mechanism: the main risk comes from correlation across major producing regions, where multiple partial failures interact to destabilize food-system assumptions.

Best use: testing systemic food-risk, reserve design, import contingency planning, and exposure to synchronized shocks.

### 3. Hydrologic design failure

Primary artifact: `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-hydrologic-whiplash-and-design-failure.md`

Core mechanism: the main risk comes from water and infrastructure systems being designed for an older distribution of wet and dry conditions.

Best use: testing how non-stationarity breaks standards, operating rules, and infrastructure planning.

## Comparison

### Distinctiveness

- sequencing failure is strongest when the inquiry is about institutional lag inside one place or seasonally connected system
- correlated breadbasket stress is strongest when the inquiry is about cross-region dependence and systemic transmission
- hydrologic design failure is strongest when the inquiry is about the translation of volatility into asset and rule failure

The three families are meaningfully different. They are not stylistic variants of one narrative.

## Evidence maturity

- sequencing failure currently has moderate evidence support; the mechanism is plausible, but the repo still needs stronger case material
- correlated breadbasket stress currently has the strongest direct support from the existing evidence base in the repo, especially from Gaupp et al. (2020) and Heino et al. (2023)
- hydrologic design failure has strong high-level support from IPCC findings, but still needs more basin- or city-specific grounding

## Preparedness value

- sequencing failure is useful for public communication, institutional planning, and scenario design around compounding transitions
- correlated breadbasket stress is useful for contingency planning because it points toward reserves, procurement logic, trade exposure, and unequal access
- hydrologic design failure is useful for standards review and infrastructure governance because it turns non-stationarity into a concrete planning problem

## Measurement opportunities

- sequencing failure could be evaluated by whether it generates better transition-aware planning questions than single-event narratives
- correlated breadbasket stress has the clearest near-term measurement path because it can be compared against crop, trade, reserve, and price literature
- hydrologic design failure could be evaluated by whether it surfaces concrete design assumptions that fail under shifted distributions

## Main judgment

The best family to deepen first is `correlated breadbasket stress`.

Why:

- it already has the strongest direct literature support in the repo
- it has a clearer bridge from climate mechanism to preparedness implication than the other families at this stage
- it offers a relatively legible path from broad framing to region-specific evidence without pretending to forecast a single outcome

This does not make it the most important family overall. It makes it the best current candidate for disciplined deepening.

## What the family set suggests about the method

- climate-volatility scenarios become more useful when each family is organized around one dominant failure mechanism
- the method benefits from comparing scenario families before diving into local detail
- source notes materially improve curation because they reveal where a scenario is evidence-rich versus evidence-thin

## Next move

Deepen the breadbasket family with region-specific grounding and use that work as a test of whether scenario refinement improves without collapsing into overclaim.

That deepening has now started through a wheat-focused transmission variant: `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-northern-wheat-correlation-shock.md`, and a more explicit Russia-Europe comparison variant: `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-russia-europe-wheat-trade-shock.md`.
