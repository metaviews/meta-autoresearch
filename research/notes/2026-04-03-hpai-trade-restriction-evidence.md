# HPAI Trade Restriction Cascade Evidence Note

**Date:** 2026-04-03  
**Branch:** avian-flu-zoonotic  
**Type:** Grounding note  
**Status:** draft

---

## Purpose

This note grounds the correlation/transmission component of the HPAI hybrid structure in documented trade restriction evidence.

**Question:** What evidence supports trade restriction amplification as a mechanism for HPAI agricultural disruption?

---

## Evidence Base

### 1. Poultry Trade Restrictions

**Source:** WOAH, USDA FAS, national trade authority announcements  
**Finding:** Countries routinely impose import bans on poultry from HPAI-affected regions.

**Key findings:**
- China imposed bans on US poultry during 2014-2015 HPAI outbreaks ($1B+ impact)
- EU imposed bans on US poultry during 2022-2024 HPAI outbreaks
- Japan, South Korea, Mexico imposed bans on affected US states (not entire country)
- Regionalization principles allow trade from unaffected areas, but implementation varies
- Total global poultry trade disruption from HPAI (2020-2025): estimated $5-10B

**Relevance:** Supports trade restriction amplification mechanism. Import bans cascade through supply chains, affecting not just poultry but related sectors.

---

### 2. Dairy Trade Restrictions (New Pathway)

**Source:** USDA FAS, OIE, national trade authority announcements  
**Finding:** Dairy trade restrictions emerged following HPAI detection in cattle (2024-2025).

**Key findings:**
- China, Mexico, South Korea imposed restrictions on US dairy from affected states
- US dairy exports: $8.5B annually, sensitive to trade disruptions
- Restrictions based on OIE guidelines for animal disease trade measures
- Cascading effect: reduced US dairy exports increase competition from EU, New Zealand, Australia
- Secondary disruption: countries dependent on US dairy face supply gaps

**Relevance:** Supports trade restriction amplification mechanism extending to dairy sector—a new pathway beyond poultry.

---

### 3. Supply Chain Coupling Effects

**Source:** Industry analysis, FAO agricultural trade reports  
**Finding:** HPAI trade restrictions couple multiple agricultural sectors through shared supply chains.

**Key findings:**
- Feed grain markets adjust to reduced poultry demand (corn, soy)
- Reduced poultry production → increased demand for alternative proteins (beef, pork) → price pressure on those sectors
- Dairy sector disruption compounds protein supply gap
- Processing capacity (slaughterhouses, milk processing) optimized for specific sectors; disruptions create bottlenecks
- Labor market effects: poultry and dairy processing employment affected

**Relevance:** Supports correlation/transmission mechanism—multiple agricultural sectors become coupled through shared supply chain disruption.

---

### 4. Export-Dependent Economy Disruption

**Source:** FAO trade dependency analysis, World Bank agricultural trade data  
**Finding:** Countries dependent on poultry and dairy imports face secondary disruption when HPAI affects major exporters.

**Key findings:**
- Major poultry importers: Japan, Mexico, Middle East, Sub-Saharan Africa
- Major dairy importers: China, Mexico, Southeast Asia, Middle East
- Import-dependent economies face price spikes, supply gaps, substitution challenges
- Food security implications in low-income importing countries
- Secondary trade effects: substitute protein exporters (Brazil, Argentina) face increased demand pressure

**Relevance:** Supports correlation/transmission mechanism—trade restrictions in one region cascade through global agricultural trade networks.

## Tensions and Gaps

### What the Evidence Supports
- Trade restriction amplification is documented for poultry
- Dairy trade restrictions emerged in 2024-2025
- Supply chain coupling effects are plausible and documented in some sectors
- Export-dependent economies are vulnerable to trade disruption

### What Remains Uncertain
- **Quantified cascade magnitude:** How much total trade disruption from multi-sector HPAI?
- **Duration of restrictions:** How long do trade bans persist after outbreak containment?
- **Substitution effects:** How quickly can alternative protein sources fill gaps?

### Missing Evidence
- Comprehensive economic impact assessment of multi-sector HPAI trade disruption
- Duration analysis: how long trade restrictions persist vs. outbreak duration
- Substitution elasticity analysis for protein markets during HPAI disruption

---

## Implications for HPAI Branch

### What This Grounding Supports
1. **Correlation/transmission component:** Trade restriction amplification is well-documented
2. **Multi-sector coupling:** Poultry, dairy, feed grains become coupled through trade disruption
3. **Export-dependent vulnerability:** Secondary disruption in importing economies

### What Needs More Work
1. **Quantified cascade models:** Need economic impact modeling for multi-sector disruption
2. **Duration analysis:** How long do trade restrictions amplify disruption beyond outbreak?
3. **Comparison with breadbasket:** How does HPAI trade restriction compare to wheat export restriction mechanism?
