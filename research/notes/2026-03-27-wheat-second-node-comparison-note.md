## Purpose

Compare Europe, China, and North America as the second major stress node for the northern wheat shock branch and select the best next comparison variant.

## Comparison question

If Russia is the transmission anchor because of export restriction risk and Black Sea trade influence, which second node best deepens the scenario by testing reduced substitutability across the global wheat system?

## Candidate 1: Europe

### Why it matters

Europe is the strongest like-for-like comparison with Russia because it is both a major wheat-growing region and a major trade-relevant wheat node.

### Evidence support

- Heino et al. (2023) support growing hot-dry wheat stress across northern-latitude production regions, including Europe.
- The European Commission cereals page states that the EU is one of the world's largest cereals producers and traders, that more than half of cereals grown in the EU are wheat, and that around 20% of the EU wheat crop is exported annually.
- USDA ERS reports that by 2015-16 the Black Sea region plus the EU made up 44% of world wheat trade, and that supply shocks in the Black Sea region and the EU are increasingly important to the world wheat supply-demand outlook.

### Why this is strong

- it preserves the transmission logic of the branch: Russia is impaired and one of the main substitute export buffers is also weakened
- it strengthens the trade and price dimension without losing the climate dimension

## Candidate 2: China

### Why it matters

China is highly relevant as a production and demand buffer. It is a strong climate-exposure comparison but a weaker export-transmission comparison.

### Evidence support

- Heino et al. (2023) report larger wheat yield reductions in relatively cool regions including China.
- USDA FAS reporting shows that China's wheat system matters primarily for domestic balancing rather than as a direct wheat-export transmission node.

### Limitation

- China deepens the physical-stress side of the branch more than the trade-substitution side

## Candidate 3: North America

### Why it matters

North America is a genuine export alternative and remains very important to world wheat supply.

### Limitation

- the current open-access evidence set here is weaker on the specific comparison logic than for Europe
- it is a plausible later comparison case, but not the cleanest next move from the current branch

## Selection

The best next second node is `Europe`.

Why:

- it is the cleanest comparison with Russia on trade relevance
- it gives the branch a stronger test of reduced substitutability across export-capable wheat regions
- it supports a sharper preparedness question around reserves, procurement, and price formation

## What this note changes

- it shifts the branch from a generic `Russia plus another region` framing toward a clearer `Russia plus Europe` comparison variant
- it keeps China active as a future production-buffer comparison, not as the best immediate transmission comparison

## Caution

- do not claim Europe is definitively the most climate-correlated partner with Russia from the current evidence alone
- do not treat China as irrelevant; it remains important for later comparison work
- do not imply that a Russia-plus-Europe stress automatically causes global wheat collapse; the stronger claim is tighter substitution, thinner buffers, and higher procurement stress
