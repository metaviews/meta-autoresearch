## Purpose

This note grounds the design/rule conflict component of the HPAI hybrid structure in biosecurity protocol inadequacy evidence.

**Question:** What evidence supports biosecurity framework mismatch as a mechanism for HPAI agricultural disruption?

---

## Evidence Base

### 1. Poultry Biosecurity Limitations

**Source:** USDA APHIS, WOAH, peer-reviewed biosecurity studies  
**Finding:** Poultry biosecurity is effective for contained outbreaks but inadequate for wild bird reservoir.

**Key findings:**
- Standard poultry biosecurity: exclude wild birds, control access, disinfect equipment
- Effectiveness: reduces but does not eliminate wild bird contamination risk
- Wild bird reservoir creates ongoing environmental contamination
- Biosecurity assumes identifiable source and containment endpoint; HPAI with wild bird reservoir has neither
- Cost-benefit: biosecurity improvements have diminishing returns when environmental contamination is ongoing

**Relevance:** Supports design/rule conflict mechanism. Poultry biosecurity designed for contained outbreaks faces pathogen with wild bird reservoir.

---

### 2. Dairy Biosecurity Limitations

**Source:** USDA APHIS, veterinary biosecurity guidelines  
**Finding:** Dairy biosecurity designed for bacterial threats (brucellosis, TB) may be inadequate for viral spillover.

**Key findings:**
- Standard dairy biosecurity: test for bacterial diseases, quarantine infected animals, cull severely affected
- Viral threats differ from bacterial: faster transmission, environmental persistence, wild reservoir
- HPAI in cattle: transmission routes not fully understood (aerosol? feed? water? equipment?)
- Cattle-to-cattle transmission documented but R0 not well quantified
- Current cattle biosecurity protocols adapted from bacterial disease framework, not viral

**Relevance:** Supports design/rule conflict mechanism. Dairy biosecurity designed for bacterial threats faces viral spillover with different transmission dynamics.

---

### 3. Wildlife Biosecurity Limitations

**Source:** WOAH wildlife disease guidelines, wildlife authority reports  
**Finding:** Wildlife biosecurity frameworks designed for contained outbreaks face pathogen with wild bird reservoir.

**Key findings:**
- Standard wildlife biosecurity: identify outbreak source, quarantine affected population, monitor for clearance
- HPAI with wild bird reservoir: source is ongoing (wild bird migration), quarantine impossible, clearance cannot be declared
- Sea lion response (Peru, Chile 2023): standard outbreak protocols applied (carcass removal, monitoring)
- Protocols effective for contained wildlife diseases (rabies, distemper) but inadequate for ongoing reintroduction risk
- No framework exists for managing wildlife disease with environmental reservoir

**Relevance:** Supports design/rule conflict mechanism. Wildlife biosecurity designed for contained outbreaks faces pathogen with environmental reservoir.

---

### 4. Trade Biosecurity Limitations

**Source:** OIE trade guidelines, national trade authority reports  
**Finding:** Trade restriction frameworks designed for contained outbreaks face pathogen with global distribution.

**Key findings:**
- Standard trade biosecurity: ban imports from affected regions, resume when outbreak contained
- HPAI with wild bird reservoir: outbreak cannot be "contained" in wild bird populations
- Regionalization principles allow trade from unaffected areas, but wild bird migration makes regional status uncertain
- Trade restrictions persist beyond poultry outbreak containment due to ongoing wild bird risk
- No framework exists for trade management with ongoing environmental contamination risk

**Relevance:** Supports design/rule conflict mechanism. Trade biosecurity designed for contained outbreaks faces pathogen with global distribution.

## Tensions and Gaps

### What the Evidence Supports
- Biosecurity frameworks designed for contained outbreaks are inadequate for wild bird reservoir
- Each biosecurity domain (poultry, dairy, wildlife, trade) faces different aspects of the same mismatch
- No framework exists for managing disease with environmental reservoir
- Standard protocols have diminishing returns when environmental contamination is ongoing

### What Remains Uncertain
- **Biosecurity effectiveness:** How much does current biosecurity reduce risk, even if inadequate?
- **Framework redesign:** What would adequate biosecurity look like for wild bird reservoir?
- **Cost-effectiveness:** Is biosecurity improvement worth the investment given ongoing reintroduction risk?

### Missing Evidence
- Comprehensive biosecurity gap analysis for HPAI with wild bird reservoir
- Framework redesign proposals for environmental reservoir management
- Cost-effectiveness analysis of biosecurity improvements vs. alternative strategies
