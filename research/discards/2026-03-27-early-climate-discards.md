## What was considered

This record captures several early directions that were implicitly left behind as the `whiplash` and `breadbasket` branches became clearer.

## Direction 1: Generic food-crisis framing

### Why it seemed promising

Broad food-crisis language initially looked useful because it expressed the stakes of correlated climate stress quickly and intuitively.

### Why it was not kept as-is

- it often lacked a clear volatility mechanism
- it blurred physical crop stress with policy and market amplification
- it implied uniform global scarcity too easily

### What changed the judgment

The breadbasket branch became stronger once it was reframed around correlation, transmission, buffers, and unequal downstream exposure rather than a generic food-crisis story.

### Disposition

- discard: broad food-crisis phrasing as a primary scenario form
- merge into: `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-synchronous-breadbasket-stress.md`
- hold for later: no
- conditions for revival: only if used as a downstream consequence layer tied to a clearer mechanism

### Method lesson

High-stakes language is not a substitute for structural clarity.

## Direction 2: Overbroad whiplash hazard bundling

### Why it seemed promising

Early multi-hazard sequence language felt generative because it made institutional lag visible and opened a wide scenario space.

### Why it was not kept as-is

- it bundled too many hazards into one narrative
- it risked sounding insightful without being testable
- it did not distinguish sequence failure from generic compounding risk clearly enough

### What changed the judgment

The parent whiplash scenario improved only after being narrowed to a wet-to-hot-dry transition family and then translated into named variants.

### Disposition

- discard: broad unbounded multi-hazard whiplash language as a final artifact
- merge into: `research/scenarios/2026-03-27-compound-seasonal-whiplash.md`
- hold for later: no
- conditions for revival: only if a future branch can show a tighter and better evidenced transition logic

### Method lesson

Sequence language becomes useful only when the transition itself is bounded enough to evaluate.

## Direction 3: Flat branch equality

### Why it seemed promising

Early in the repo, it was tempting to treat every emerging family as equally worth developing in order to preserve openness.

### Why it was not kept as-is

- it weakened curation pressure
- it made stronger branches harder to prioritize
- it confused exploratory openness with reluctance to judge

### What changed the judgment

The evaluation matrix and branch comparisons made it possible to keep weaker directions provisionally while still assigning different maturity and priority.

### Disposition

- discard: implicit equal-status treatment of all branches
- merge into: explicit `keep` / `revise` / `merge` / `discard` logic in `docs/evaluation-framework.md`
- hold for later: no
- conditions for revival: none; this is a method error, not a branch worth revisiting

### Method lesson

Openness without pruning quickly becomes noise.

## Overall method lesson

Discard records should be created earlier. The repo has already benefited from pruning, but much of that pruning has remained implicit. Making it explicit will improve honesty, repeatability, and later scale-readiness.
