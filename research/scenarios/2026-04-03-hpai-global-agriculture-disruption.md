## Research question

How might highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) H5N1 create global agricultural disruption not mainly through human pandemic, but through zoonotic transmission pathways that couple wild bird ecology, poultry production, mammalian adaptation, and trade amplification into a correlated disruption of multiple agricultural sectors simultaneously?

## Why this scenario exists

This is the first branch outside climate, political economy, and health security (wealth-concentration pandemic variant). HPAI tests whether the method can handle biological volatility—a different driver from climate non-stationarity or financial concentration.

HPAI is currently causing unprecedented agricultural disruption without human pandemic. This scenario explores whether the structure type is correlation (like breadbasket), sequence (like whiplash), design/rule conflict (like hydrologic), or hybrid.

## Framing and assumptions

- baseline assumptions:
  - HPAI H5N1 is now enzootic in wild bird populations across 60+ countries
  - Mammalian adaptation is occurring (cattle, sea lions, other mammals)
  - Poultry is the most consumed meat globally (~130M tonnes annually)
  - Trade restrictions amplify localized outbreaks into global disruption
- assumptions that depart from default narratives:
  - the main disruption pathway may not be human pandemic but agricultural disruption at current transmission levels
  - wild bird reservoir makes eradication impossible with current tools
  - multiple agricultural sectors (poultry, dairy, feed grains) become coupled through shared disruption pathways
- boundaries of the scenario:
  - this is a structural stress scenario, not a pandemic forecast
  - the focus is on agricultural disruption pathways, not human mortality

## Scenario logic

Wild bird migration spreads HPAI H5N1 across continents. Poultry operations in the path experience outbreaks requiring mass culling. The virus spills into mammals: first sea lions in South America (20,000+ deaths), then cattle in North America (US dairy herds, 2024-2025), potentially other livestock.

The agricultural disruption is not confined to poultry. Dairy production is affected when cattle become infected. Feed grain markets adjust to reduced poultry demand, affecting global grain balances. Trade restrictions cascade: countries ban imports from affected regions, creating secondary disruptions in export-dependent economies.

The preparedness failure lies in treating each sector independently—poultry biosecurity, dairy biosecurity, grain markets, trade policy—as if they were separate problems. The virus couples them through shared transmission pathways.

## Grounding

- WHO, WOAH, FAO report H5N1 in 60+ countries across all continents except Antarctica
- Sea lion outbreaks in Peru and Chile (2023): 20,000+ deaths, mammal-to-mammal transmission
- US dairy herds affected (2024-2025): H5N1 detected across multiple states
- First documented cattle-to-human transmission (US dairy worker, 2024)
- PB2 E627K and D701N mutations detected in mammalian infections (mammalian adaptation signals)
- ~900 human infections since 2003, ~50% case fatality rate

## Signals and evidence classes

- signals already visible:
  - HPAI geographic spread unprecedented in avian influenza history
  - Mammalian adaptation mutations detected
  - Cattle outbreaks creating new transmission pathway
  - Trade restrictions amplifying local outbreaks
- evidence classes consulted:

## Uncertainties and failure modes

- key uncertainties:
  - how close is virus to efficient human-to-human transmission?
  - how established is cattle-to-cattle transmission?
  - can wild bird reservoir be managed with current tools?
- where this could be misleading:
  - could overstate agricultural disruption if current outbreaks remain contained
  - could understate if virus acquires efficient human transmission
  - could conflate biological dynamics with social response dynamics
- what would challenge the scenario most:
  - evidence that agricultural biosecurity is effectively containing outbreaks
  - evidence that trade restrictions are proportionate rather than amplifying
